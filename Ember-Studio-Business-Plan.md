## The offer — two ways to work with the studio

### 1. Launch Sprint — one-time (the front door + fast cash)

Everything a startup needs to look Series-A, shipped in ~1–2 weeks:

- **Premium marketing website** — Linear / Vercel / Stripe tier. Designed, built, deployed.
- **Brand system** — marks, palette, type, guidelines. Refined from existing or built new.
- **Positioning & messaging** — the hero, the narrative, the story that makes them look inevitable.
- **One embedded AI agent** — a support agent that resolves tickets, an onboarding agent that gets users to value, or a workflow-automation agent, built *inside their product*. (A real agent that does something — not a chatbot bolted to the corner.)

**Price: £4,000–£6,000** one-time. Premium, but a funded founder can say yes on the call.

### 2. The Continuum — recurring (the MRR engine)

Your web + AI team on tap. **One active request at a time, unlimited requests, pause or cancel anytime.** The productised model — no scoping wars, no proposals.

Ongoing work: new landing / launch / feature pages · site iteration · AI agents & automations · dashboards & internal tools · hosting, performance & maintenance.

**Price: £2,500–£3,500/mo.**

> **This is how you hit 5-figure MRR "easily": 4 clients × £3,000 = £12,000 MRR.** Four clients. Premium price. Recurring. That's the whole game.

**The flow:** Launch Sprint → roll into the Continuum. The Sprint proves the standard and pays the bills; the Continuum compounds into MRR.

Two real layers, both true:

1. **AI as method** — you ship premium in *days* because you build AI-accelerated. That's the speed moat agencies can't match.
2. **AI as deliverable** — you build AI *agents* into client products: support agents, onboarding agents, workflow automations, internal tools. Startups want agents and have no bandwidth to build them. You supply it.

## The Trojan horse → Ember Forge (the endgame)

The service is not the destination — it's how you fund and design the product.

Every subscription client throws the same requests at you over and over. That repetition *is* the product spec. Once you're running 5–10 clients, you'll know exactly what to build: **Ember Forge** — a tool that lets startups spin up and maintain premium, on-brand web (and later, AI agents) themselves.

Same buyer the whole way through. Service funds product. Product eventually eats the manual delivery. You can code it yourself, so this is real — not a someday-dream.

## First cash in weeks — the sprint

You need income *now*, so this is aggressive and doable.

**The target list (build it this week — aim for 50):**
Startups that *just raised* or are *about to launch*. Public, timeable triggers — funding announcements on LinkedIn & tech press, Crunchbase, Product Hunt "upcoming," "we raised" posts on X. Money in the bank + acute need to look legit.

**The hook (your reveal model, rebuilt for startups):**
Pick a target. Spend 60–90 min doing an AI-accelerated redesign of their hero / landing page. Record a 2-min Loom: *"I redesigned your landing — here's the before/after, no strings."* Design-led founders cannot resist seeing their own site made premium. It's your spec-build reveal, modernised — and it shows your taste before you say a word.

**Channels:** X/Twitter DMs (where founders live) · LinkedIn · warm intros · founder/indie communities. No cold calls. No walk-ins.

**Daily targets:**
- 3–5 tailored Loom teardowns
- 20 DMs to freshly-funded / launching founders
- 5 follow-ups
- 1 portfolio piece shipped (until the portfolio is strong)

**Goal: first paying client (Sprint or Subscription) within 21 days.**

## The one real risk — name it, beat it

**Taste is the entire moat.** Startups are the most sophisticated web/design buyers alive. A generic build dies here. You win only if the work is genuinely world-class — so the portfolio has to prove it before the outreach lands.

**Fix, week one:** build 3 concept redesigns of recognisable startups (or make your own studio site the flagship). Offer the first 1–2 clients a founder rate in exchange for a case study. Get proof on the board fast, then charge full price.

## 90-day roadmap

**Weeks 1–2 — Set the stage.** Lock brand & positioning. Build the portfolio (3 concepts + flagship studio site). Build the 50-target list. Set up Loom-teardown outreach. Start sending.

**Weeks 2–4 — First blood.** Outreach at volume. Land the first Launch Sprint. Deliver it flawlessly → first case study.

**Weeks 8–12 — Stabilise & spot the product.** 4+ Continuum clients = £10k+ MRR. Systemise delivery. Start sketching Forge from the patterns.

**Beyond — Build Forge.** With your clients as design partners and the service funding it.
